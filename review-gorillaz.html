The Critic's Read Editorial analysis
Gorillaz have always been less a band than a delivery system for a specific kind of emotional weather — melancholy that doesn't announce itself, joy that arrives sideways. The Mountain continues that tradition with enough precision to satisfy the committed listener and enough strangeness to keep the casual one at arm's length.
"An 8.0 on Emotional Depth and Production in the same analysis is not a contradiction. It's a portrait of an album that builds its world with care and then populates it unevenly."
The Signal · People's Score Analysis
What the audience data surfaces most clearly is the split between listeners who came to this album carrying history and listeners who came without it. The Demon Days / Plastic Beach generation scores this record significantly higher across every dimension — not because they're being generous, but because they have the context the album quietly assumes. Without that context, Thematic Consistency drops to 6.0, and the architecture that feels intentional to the devotee feels incomplete to the newcomer.
The 7.4 is an honest number. It reflects an album that achieved exactly what it set out to do for a specific audience — and is less legible to everyone else. That is, for Gorillaz, essentially the whole point.

What the Clusters Say Audience signal analysis
The Emotional Listeners cluster is the most analytically interesting in this dataset. These are people who appear to have processed personal grief, transition, or loss through this album — and their signal is disproportionately strong relative to cluster size. Their 8.0 on Emotional Depth reflects something closer to testimony than review.
"The Legacy Comparators are the loudest friction in the data — and the most predictable. Every Gorillaz album since Plastic Beach has been measured against Plastic Beach. The Mountain does not escape that gravity."
Legacy Comparators · Cluster Signal
The Visual / Animation Fans cluster is the quiet surprise. These listeners scored the album's Aesthetic dimension highest of any cluster, and their engagement patterns suggest they are experiencing The Mountain as much as watching it — which aligns with how Gorillaz has always intended its work to land.
Fan Engagement at 6.0 is the score that needs watching. It does not indicate disengagement — it indicates a split between audiences who feel spoken to and audiences who feel observed. That split is the album's central fault line.
The Verdict Final signal read
A 7.4 for Gorillaz in 2026 is not a step back. It is a precise measurement of an album that chose its audience deliberately, built a world for them with genuine craft, and accepted the cost of that choice in reach and universality.
The Mountain is the album Gorillaz needed to make. Whether it's the album the wider audience needed to hear is a question the data answers honestly: for some, completely. For others, not yet.
"7.4 / 10. The production is immaculate. The emotion is real. The thesis is present but not always legible. All of that is, somehow, very Gorillaz."
