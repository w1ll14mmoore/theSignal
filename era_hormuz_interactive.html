Strategic Context
The numbers that make closure unthinkable
Seventeen million barrels of crude oil transit the strait daily — more than the combined output of Iraq, Kuwait, and the UAE. A 30-day closure would drain the US Strategic Petroleum Reserve by 40%.

Japan imports 87% of its oil through Hormuz. South Korea imports 76%. India, 65%. A closure is not a regional crisis — it is a global economic shock within 48 hours.

The US Fifth Fleet, headquartered in Bahrain, maintains continuous carrier presence in the region precisely because the math of closure is too catastrophic to contemplate. The strait is both the world's greatest vulnerability and its most heavily defended chokepoint.
